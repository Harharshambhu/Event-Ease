Module 3: Assets & inventory — equipment request, approval & billback
Phase 1 — Configuration (admin)
ElementsTasksObjectives
Asset catalogAll requestable equipment Define available assets: golf carts, walkie talkies, radios, tables, chairs, tents, forklifts, storage units, vehicles, signage, power drops — any physical item. Set total inventory quantity per item Create a single source of truth for what's available to allocate — no more over-promising across departments
Pricing per itemFor billback to vendors Set rental/usage price per asset item. Prices can vary by group type or be flat. Some items may be free (included) while others are charged Enable vendor billback — charge vendors for golf carts and radios they use, turning assets from cost center to revenue
Item blocksRequest packages per group type Bundle assets into requestable packages with quantity limits. Assign blocks to group type portals (e.g., vendors can request carts and radios; artists cannot) Control which stakeholders can request which equipment — prevent artists from ordering forklifts
Inventory limitsPer-item and per-block caps Set maximum requestable quantity per item per group, and enforce total inventory ceiling across all groups Prevent over-allocation — if you have 30 golf carts, the system won't approve 35 across all vendors
Phase 2 — Request & collection (stakeholders)
ElementsTasksObjectives
Portal asset requestSelf-service ordering Stakeholder selects from available items in their portal, enters quantity needed, adds notes (e.g., "need cart delivered to Stage B by 6 AM") Push the ordering burden to the requester — they know what they need better than the admin does
Custom request formsFor complex or special needs For non-standard requests (custom signage, special equipment), stakeholder fills out a form describing the need Capture edge-case requests without forcing them into a rigid catalog — flexibility within structure
Request visibilityReal-time status in portal Stakeholder sees their request status: submitted, pending review, approved (with quantity), denied (with reason) Eliminate "did you get my golf cart request?" emails — self-service status kills the back-and-forth
Phase 3 — Review, approval & allocation (admin)
ElementsTasksObjectives
Approval queueAll pending asset requests Review requests across all groups. Approve full quantity, partial quantity, or deny. Add notes to explain decisions Allocate limited resources fairly — when 5 vendors want 30 carts and you have 20, the admin decides who gets what
Allocation dashboardTotal inventory vs. committed View total inventory per asset type, how much is allocated (approved), how much is pending, and remaining available Real-time visibility into resource utilization — know at a glance if you're about to over-commit golf carts
Group-level asset reportWhat each group has been approved Export per-group asset allocation reports. Used for delivery logistics, onsite distribution, and post-event reconciliation Give operations team a clear delivery manifest — Acme AV gets 3 carts at Gate B, StageWorks gets 2 forklifts at Dock C
Billback / paymentCharge vendors for equipment Generate invoices based on approved asset orders. Process payment via Stripe integration. Track paid vs. outstanding Turn asset management from a cost center to a revenue stream — vendors pay for what they use
Phase 4 — Onsite & post-event
ElementsTasksObjectives
Onsite dispute resolutionSingle source of truth When a vendor says "I was approved for 5 radios" and operations says "we show 3," the dashboard settles it instantly End onsite arguments with data — the approved record is timestamped and visible to both parties
Return trackingPost-event reconciliation Mark assets as returned. Identify missing or damaged equipment per group Hold groups accountable for equipment — know who didn't return the golf cart
Year-over-year dataCross-event CRM persistence Historical asset request data carries forward. Admin can see "Acme AV ordered 3 carts last year — they'll probably need the same" Smarter forecasting and procurement based on actual usage patterns, not guesswork
