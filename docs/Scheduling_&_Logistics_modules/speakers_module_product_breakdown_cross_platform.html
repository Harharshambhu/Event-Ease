Module: Speakers (call for speakers) — submission, review, onboarding & management
Lennd Portal-based: bios, docs, schedules via group portal Zoho Website-integrated: speaker profiles, session delivery, analytics Cvent Enterprise-grade: abstract management, peer review, Speaker Resource Center
Phase 1 — Call for speakers / abstracts (collecting proposals)
ElementsTasksObjectives
Call for papers / proposals pagePublic submission portal Create a branded submission page where prospective speakers submit proposals. Cvent Abstract Management offers fully customizable submission forms with tailored fields, file upload support, and mobile-responsive design. Zoho is building abstract management for 2026 (currently uses forms + manual workflow). Lennd uses its form builder to create call-for-speaker forms assigned to a "Speaker" group type portal Cast a wide net — attract the best speakers by making submission easy, professional, and accessible from any device
Submission form fieldsWhat you collect from each proposal Configure required fields: session title, abstract/description, topic track, session format (keynote/panel/workshop/lightning talk), speaker bio, headshot, social links, AV requirements, co-presenters, presentation file upload. Cvent supports custom field types and conditional logic per track. Lennd supports custom fields via the form builder with file uploads. Zoho uses registration forms with custom fields Collect everything the review committee needs to evaluate proposals — title, abstract, speaker credentials, and topic fit — in a structured, comparable format
Multiple submission tracksSeparate calls for different content types Run parallel calls: "Call for keynotes," "Call for workshops," "Call for posters," "Call for panels" — each with different form fields, deadlines, and review criteria. Cvent supports multiple submission types within one Abstract Management instance. Zoho supports multi-session types. Lennd can create separate forms per submission category A keynote proposal needs different information than a poster submission — separate tracks with tailored forms ensure you collect the right data for each content type
Submission deadline managementOpen/close dates, extensions Set open and close dates for submissions. Configure automated reminder emails as deadlines approach. Optionally extend deadlines for specific tracks or late submissions. Cvent has automated email workflows for deadline reminders. Lennd portals have configurable close dates with automated reminders. Zoho supports deadline-driven email campaigns Create urgency while maintaining flexibility — deadlines drive submissions, but extensions handle the reality that the best speakers are often the busiest and submit late
Submission confirmation & trackingSubmitter sees their status After submission, speaker receives confirmation email and can log in to check status: submitted, under review, accepted, rejected, revision requested. Cvent sends automated status notifications at each stage. Lennd shows application status in the speaker's portal. Zoho sends registration confirmation emails Reduce "did you get my submission?" emails to zero — speakers self-check status, freeing the content team from inbox management
Phase 2 — Review & selection (content team / review committee)
ElementsTasksObjectives
Reviewer assignmentWho evaluates which proposals Assign internal team members or external advisory board to review submissions. Cvent allows assigning reviewers by topic/track, granting portal access to rate and comment. Supports conflict-of-interest flagging. Lennd uses the applications module with admin grading/scoring. Zoho uses internal team collaboration for session curation (no formal review portal yet) Distribute the review workload fairly — the AI track chair reviews AI submissions, the leadership track chair reviews leadership submissions. No one reviews their own company's proposal
Scoring / rating rubricStructured evaluation criteria Define scoring criteria: topic relevance (1-5), speaker expertise (1-5), audience fit (1-5), originality (1-5). Reviewers rate each submission against the rubric. Cvent supports custom scoring criteria with weighted averages, real-time score tracking, votes, and comments per submission. Lennd applications module supports grading of submissions by admin team Replace subjective "I liked that one" decisions with structured, comparable scoring — when 200 proposals compete for 40 slots, data-driven selection is the only fair and efficient path
Reviewer portal / dashboardWhere reviewers do their work Reviewers log in to a dedicated portal showing their assigned submissions, scoring interface, and progress tracker. Cvent has a full reviewer portal with sort/filter by score, vote, comment, and track. Can view submissions in list or detail mode. Lennd uses the admin dashboard with application review filters. Zoho uses team collaboration features within the platform (less structured) Give reviewers a focused workspace — not buried in a shared spreadsheet or email chain, but a purpose-built interface where they score, comment, and move to the next submission
Accept / reject / revise decisionsFinal selection workflow Based on aggregate scores, content team makes final decisions: accept, reject, request revision, waitlist. Cvent supports bulk accept/reject with automated notification emails to submitters at each decision stage. Lennd application status flows back to the applicant's portal. Zoho manages speakers individually with manual status updates Close the selection loop cleanly — every submitter knows their outcome, accepted speakers move into the onboarding pipeline, and rejected proposals get a professional notification
Waitlist managementBackup speakers for cancellations Maintain a ranked waitlist of near-accepted proposals. When a confirmed speaker cancels, promote the next waitlisted proposal. Cvent supports waitlisting within the abstract workflow. Lennd and Zoho handle this via manual status management Never scramble when a speaker drops out — the backup plan is already ranked, reviewed, and ready to slot in
Phase 3 — Speaker onboarding & content collection (accepted speakers)
ElementsTasksObjectives
Speaker Resource Center / portalSelf-service hub for confirmed speakers Accepted speakers get access to a dedicated portal where they manage their profile, upload materials, and view their schedule. Cvent has a dedicated Speaker Resource Center (SRC) — a branded self-service portal for speakers to manage bio, headshot, presentations, and session details. Lennd uses its portal system — speakers (as a group type) get a portal with forms, files, schedule, and messages. Zoho provides a speaker console where speakers upload presentations and manage their session Speakers manage themselves — upload their own bio update, swap their headshot, submit their presentation deck — without emailing the event team for every change
Profile & bio collectionSpeaker page content Collect / update: full bio, short bio, headshot (high-res), job title, organization, social links (LinkedIn, Twitter), topic tags. Zoho speaker profiles auto-publish to the event website with sessions linked. Cvent SRC collects all profile data and pushes to the Attendee Hub. Lennd collects via CRM fields and portal forms Build the "speaker lineup" page for marketing — every speaker's photo, bio, and session appears on the event website, driving registrations and establishing event credibility
Presentation / materials uploadSlides, handouts, supporting docs Speakers upload their presentation files, handouts, and supplementary materials to their portal. Zoho speakers upload presentations directly to Backstage — enabling projector-less session delivery (presentations served from the platform). Cvent SRC supports file uploads with version tracking. Lennd uses document/file request and collection via the portal Centralize all presentation materials — no more "can you email me your slides?" on event morning. Everything is uploaded, reviewed, and ready before the speaker arrives
Logistics data collectionTravel, AV, dietary, accommodation Collect speaker logistics: travel details (arrival/departure), AV requirements (microphone type, screen setup, demo needs), dietary restrictions, accommodation preferences, ground transport needs. Lennd handles this comprehensively via portal forms — speakers submit the same credential, catering, and asset requests as other group types. Cvent SRC collects logistics data alongside content. Zoho uses custom registration fields The speaker's operational needs (hotel, airport pickup, vegan lunch, lapel mic) are captured alongside their content needs — one portal, one submission, both the content team and operations team get what they need
Credential & access issuancePasses for speaker + their entourage Issue speaker credentials (all-access, greenroom, VIP) and manage their guest list allocation (e.g., 2 guest passes per speaker). Lennd excels here — speakers' credential and guest list requests flow through the same modules as vendors, with portal-based self-service. Cvent handles speaker passes via event registration. Zoho manages speaker badges via the badge printing system Speakers are VIP stakeholders who also need credentials, meals, and access — treating them through the same operational pipeline as vendors (but with elevated access levels) ensures nothing falls through the cracks
Phase 4 — Event-day delivery & post-event (live + analytics)
ElementsTasksObjectives
Session delivery toolsLive presentation support Support speakers during their live session with in-platform tools. Zoho Backstage OnAir enables projector-less delivery — speakers present directly from the platform with live streaming, screen sharing, whiteboard, polls, and Q&A built in. Cvent Attendee Hub supports live and on-demand sessions with engagement tools. Lennd does not include live session delivery tools (operations-focused, not content-delivery-focused) For hybrid and virtual events: speakers present without needing separate Zoom/Teams — one platform handles the presentation, the audience engagement, and the recording
Live audience engagementPolls, Q&A, chat during sessions During a session, attendees interact via live polls, Q&A queues, chat, and reactions. Zoho supports polls, Q&A, and chat within sessions with real-time results displayed. Cvent Attendee Hub has live Q&A, polling, and chat. Lennd does not include audience engagement tools Transform passive listening into active participation — engagement data also tells organizers which sessions generated the most interaction and which fell flat
Session recording & on-demandContent capture for post-event access Record sessions for on-demand viewing after the event. Zoho OnAir records sessions for replay. Cvent supports on-demand content libraries in the Attendee Hub. Lennd does not include recording capabilities Extend event ROI beyond the live dates — recorded sessions become content assets for marketing, member libraries, continuing education credits, and sponsor value
Speaker performance analyticsRatings, attendance, engagement data Post-event, speakers receive their performance data. Zoho provides session analytics with attendee feedback visible to speakers so they can improve. Cvent pushes survey results back to the Speaker Resource Center — speakers see how attendees rated them. Lennd does not include speaker performance analytics Close the feedback loop for speakers — they see their ratings, read attendee comments, and understand what worked. Better-informed speakers deliver better sessions next year
Speaker CRM / reuse across eventsBuilding a speaker database Maintain a database of past, current, and prospective speakers across events. Track speaking history, ratings, topic expertise, and availability. Cvent + Sessionboard integration maintains a speaker CRM that reuses speaker data across events without re-entry. Lennd cross-event CRM persists speaker records across years. Zoho integrates with Zoho CRM for contact persistence Build organizational memory — "who spoke at our 2024 conference and was rated 4.8/5 on AI topics?" becomes a searchable query, not a question that requires emailing five people who might remember
Cross-platform positioning summary: Cvent has the deepest speaker/abstract workflow — full peer review, scoring rubrics, automated notifications, Speaker Resource Center, and session analytics. It's built for enterprise conferences with 200+ submissions and multi-reviewer committees. Zoho is strongest on attendee-facing delivery — the agenda builder, speaker profiles on the event website, live session tools via OnAir, and audience engagement. Abstract management is coming in 2026. Lennd treats speakers as another stakeholder type flowing through its portal system — weaker on content curation but strongest on operational logistics (credentials, catering, asset requests for speakers and their crew). A comprehensive platform would need Cvent's review engine + Zoho's delivery/engagement tools + Lennd's operational portal.
